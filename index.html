1) Vegetable Biryani

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, potatoes), chopped
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cardamom, cloves)
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • Ghee or vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

1. Cook soaked basmati rice until it's 70-80%. Drain and set aside.
2. In a pan, heat ghee or oil. Add whole spices and sliced onions. Sauté until onions are golden brown.
3. Add ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, turmeric powder, and red chili powder. Cook for 1-2 minutes.
4. Add chopped vegetables and tomatoes. Cook until vegetables are slightly tender.
5. In a separate bowl, mix yogurt with salt. Add this mixture to the vegetables and cook for a few minutes.
6. In a large pot, layer half of the partially cooked rice, followed by the vegetable mixture. Top with the remaining rice.
7. Sprinkle fresh coriander and mint leaves over the rice. Cover and cook on low heat until the rice is fully cooked and aromatic.
8. Serve hot with raita or a side salad.

2) Butter Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

1. In a bowl, marinate chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour.
2. Heat 2 tablespoons of butter in a pan. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
3. Add marinated chicken and cook until it's no longer pink.
4. Stir in tomato puree and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
5. Add heavy cream, kasuri methi, and remaining butter. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan or rice.

3) Aloo Gobi

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium-sized cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder/li>
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Cooking oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

1. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
2. Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until onions become golden brown.
3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until the raw aroma disappears.
4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil begins to separate.
5. Stir in turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
6. Add diced potatoes and cauliflower florets. Mix to coat the vegetables with the spice mixture.
7. Cover the pan and cook on low-medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender.
8. Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
9. Serve hot with roti or rice.
